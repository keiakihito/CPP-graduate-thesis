## Slide 29 — Conclusion
*(~1 min)*

To close: we evaluated five pretrained audio models across two architecture families and three capacity tiers in a cold-start classical music retrieval setting.

The core finding is that capacity scaling is non-monotonic and task-dependent. No single model wins on both tasks. The largest Transformer carries 25 times the extraction overhead of the smallest CNN, with no ranking gain on either task.

In small, cold-start settings, increasing model capacity does not reliably improve ranking quality — but it significantly increases cost. Model selection is a cost-quality trade-off, not a pure performance optimization problem.
