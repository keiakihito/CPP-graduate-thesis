ACM Conference on Recommender Systems

The ACM Conference on Recommender Systems (ACM RecSys) is a leading annual academic and industry conference focused on recommender systems research and applications. Founded in 2007 and organized under the Association for Computing Machinery, it is widely regarded as the flagship venue dedicated specifically to recommendation technologies.
Key facts

    Type: Peer-reviewed international conference series

    First edition: 2007 (Minneapolis, USA)

    Frequency: Annual, rotating international locations

    Typical full-paper acceptance rate: Usually under 20%

    Publisher: Association for Computing Machinery (conference proceedings)

Scope and community

RecSys covers the full ecosystem of recommender systems: algorithms and models (e.g., collaborative filtering, deep learning), system design, evaluation methodologies, user studies, fairness and accountability, and real-world deployments. The community is deliberately multidisciplinary, bringing together computer scientists, data scientists, statisticians, HCI researchers, social scientists, and psychologists working on personalization and recommendation.
Status and impact

The conference holds an A ranking in the CORE conference rankings and is described as the most specialized and prestigious venue focused on recommender systems research. It attracts a substantial industry presence, with sponsors and attendees from major technology companies such as Amazon, Netflix, Google, Meta, Microsoft, and Spotify. Many accepted papers are coauthored by industry labs and have direct impact on production recommender systems used by millions of users worldwide.
Organization and series structure

Each year’s edition is hosted in a different city and led by new general chairs, but overall stewardship is provided by a permanent steering committee. Workshops, tutorials, a doctoral symposium, industrial and reproducibility tracks, and late-breaking results/posters complement the main technical program.
Related activities

RecSys also hosts the ACM RecSys Challenge, a yearly open competition centered on a practical recommendation problem (e.g., music, social media, or e-commerce recommendation). Past challenges have been organized with companies such as Twitter and Spotify, and serve as a benchmark and showcase for scalable, applied recommender-system methods.
